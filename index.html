Notable Projects

Nutrient Buddy

Image of Mock Company Logo "Friendly Farmers"


The Interdisplinary Project (IDP) is an annual organized team-based project hosted by the California Academy of Mathematics and Science for grades 9-11. The project is designed to implement the different core lessons we've learned throughout each of our pursued disciplines, and in specific, my 9th grade project, the Nutrient Buddy, focused on Computer Science as its core discipline. Nutrient Buddy seeks to offer user-friendly, accessible, and a fun interface for user to maintain their daily intake of nutrients throughout the day, week, or even month.

Nutrient Buddy began through its early stage of development through UI development on Figma, which later transitioned to the development on MIT App Inventor. It's feature include a user database of login information through storage of CSV files into an external spreadsheet database, database of catalouged meals and ingredients with their respective nutritional values, and a daily, weekly, and monthly intake summary. Lastly, one of our largest experimental implementations was the integration of an AI VLM (Vision Language Model) to identify and catalogue meals and ingredients through a user's uploaded image of their meal. (Note: The AI VLM had limited usage rates due to the limited usage rate of MIT App inventor, only being able to recognize 5 distinct meals.). We later presented this project during our school's Open House fair, finding large interest from our peers and guests through collected survey data. While largely discontinued, I am proud of the work that I've completed as the lead developer and I'd love to get an opportunity to work on this project in the future.

Robot Dog

Simple Frame and Moduled Roll Capstain drive


This is my newest and largest-scaled project that I've begun on my own, tasking myself with creating a robot dog in one year. The plan is to utilize capstain drives to replace typical gears due to the reduction of backlash and noise of the systems. The system is designed to be largely modular, allowing ease of assembly and disassembly. For example in the image above, both the frame and the modular robot legs are designed to be easily removable and attached. I am currently in the mechanical design phase, focusing on creating the essential frame design of the robot dog and maneuvering around through the different capstain drive designs. The Capstains responsible for the robot leg movements will be attached to a five bar linkage system, allowing the usage of reverse kinematics to calculate the necessary angles for the leg to reach a desired point. This project will be a interdisciplinary project, requiring all of my knowledge in mechanical, electrical, and programming to complete.

My overarching plan is to utilize my current design as a prototype and I will utilize this data to create a more refined and optimized design for the final product. One of my largest passions is the social and mental health of senior citizens, with my family owning a home for senior citizens which I often volunteer and visit at. I hope to create a robot dog that can be used to help improve the mental health of senior citizens by providing them with a companion to interact with. This will be a long-term project, and I am excited to continue working on it and testing in the future.
